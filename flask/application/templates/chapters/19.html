Donald Trump stars in ‘The Return to Power’

Saturday, June 24, 2017

Six a.m. — the hour when Donald Trump wielded his Android as a weapon of war — was rapidly approaching, so Trump pushed himself into a sitting position and grabbed for the phone. Acting President Mike Pence — and perhaps the whole nation — knew what was coming: Under the 25th Amendment, a deposed President can reclaim the Oval Office by declaring that he is fit to serve — to hell with the vice president and Cabinet members branding him him off his rocker. First step: the deposed President must deliver letters to the speaker of the House and president pro tempore of the Senate stating that he suffers from no “inability” to do the job.

Pence dialed Sen. John McCain.

Yeah, I’m on Twitter, too, Mr. Acting President, McCain said. I’m guessing that Trump’s ‘huge announcement’ isn’t that he’s making shirts that say, ‘My Vice President overthrew me and all I got was this lousy t-shirt.’

As if on cue, America’s Twitter alert flashed.

Make America Trump Again? McCain said.

Pledge? Pence said. My guess is —

Trump’s next alert silenced Pence mid-sentence.

A twinge of worry shot through Pence.

What do you think, John? he asked.

Hang in there. I’ve been counting votes all morning and I think I can deliver 67. But that’s if Schumer delivers the Democratic caucus — and he said he wants a federal investigation into the safety of stretch limos.

Tell Schumer that President Pence will make stretch limos great again, Pence said. Just get me those votes!


The T-bird is ready, sir — Reagan National, I assume, Trump’s longtime private pilot, John Dunkin, told the former President.

Yes, Steve, Trump said. But I want you to do something spectacular. On approach to Washington, I want you make a big, grand circle over the city, my city — to hell with the FAA — so the people can see that their President is coming to save the day. You can do that, right, Bill? No looking all pussified like Bush and Katrina. I want the greatest circle ever flown, loud and low, make the trees wave, scare the children so they’ll remember forever.

Will do, sir.

Make sure you tilt the plane sharp enough so everyone sees the 24-karat gold sink and toilet, Trump added.

Will do, sir, Dunkin said. One thing does concern me, sir.

“What, no!” Trump sounded panicked. Don’t tell me you forgot the KFC!

No, sir, there’s a bucket of extra crispy, with a knife and fork, in the galley. I’m only concerned because Reagan National won’t give me landing clearance.

You bet they won’t, Trump yelled. Believe me, they know we’re coming.


CNN’s weekend anchor Victor Blackwell put his finger to his ear and interrupted an interview with Sen. Charles Schumer of New York.

Excuse me, Senator, I am sorry to interrupt your call for a mandate of at least eight tater tots in every federally financed school lunch —

Well, Victor, it’s because sometimes there are seven, sometimes there are nine, and it’s very confusing to the stud— Schumer tried to butt in.

I understand that, sir, but I have breaking news: Former President Trump’s Boeing 757 has entered Washington airspace and made a broad, rooftop circle of the capital city. I go live to the big guns at CNN Center, where Jake Tapper is anchoring the coverage, Jake?

First, apologies to Sen. Schumer for cutting away from his tater-tots mandate, but this morning’s news is stunning: As you can see, former President Trump’s plane is making a wide sweep of Washington, Tapper intoned on CNN. Anderson, what do you make of that flight plan?

I’d say the former President is making a statement, Anderson Cooper responded.

Meanwhile, on MSNBC, Rachel Maddow had a different take.

Brian, she said to her colleague Brian Williams, that’s not a flight plan over Washington, it’s an ‘F-you’ to Washington.

I have to agree, Rachel, that Donald Trump may be the first person to turn an airplane itself into a middle finger, Williams said.

Fox News covered the flight in the manner that Iranian revolutionary TV covered Ayatollah Khomeini’s return from exile in 1979. He’s back! Sean Hannity practically screamed into the camera. Our very short national nightmare is over!

That’s not a flight plan over Washington, it’s an ‘F-you’ to Washington.

Ultimately, fearing an air disaster, Pence allowed Trump Force One to land at Reagan National Airport, but the deposed President’s vision of Americans eager to kiss his super-long necktie did not match reality.

The runways and roadways were lined instead by soldiers.

Sir, the welcome does not appear to be friendly, Dunkin said. The military is not allowing your limo access to the plane.

But I have to get to the Capitol, Trump said.

You’ll have to walk, sir, Dunkin said.

Walk? The only ground my feet touch are a beautifully manicured fairway — I’m never in the rough! Jim, this is an outrage.”

But then the reality TV star inside Donald Trump flickered back to life.

Unless...

Trump picked up the Android and dialed CNN President Jeff Zucker.

Listen, fake news boy, it’s the President, Trump said.

Who’s the fake news now, Mr. Trump? Zucker said. You are not, in fact, the President.

A matter of time, Zucker, Trump barked.

In any event, how can failing CNN help you today? Are you having trouble with your cable? I believe the service number for Optimum in the Palm Beach area is 800-934—

I have Fios, Zucker. It’s the best. Very fast, Trump boasted. I’m going to buy the company and call it ‘Trumpios.’ But that’s not why I’m calling....

Trump then explained that he intended to walk from Reagan National to the Capitol and was offering to let Anderson Cooper tag along.

It’s four miles from Reagan National to the Capitol. It’s hardly Mao’s brilliant retreat across 3,700 miles of China.

You’ll have all the footage of what historians will someday call ‘The Long March,’ Trump proclaimed.

Sir, it’s four miles from Reagan National to the Capitol, Zucker said. It’s hardly Mao’s brilliant retreat across 3,700 miles of China.

China! We never win anymore, Trump said. But, OK, so maybe it’s not the Long March. It’s more like that Gandhi thing.

Are you going to make salt when you get to the Capitol, sir? Zucker asked sarcastically. Are you going to wear a loincloth along the way?

You’re such a bastard, Zucker. So dishonest, Trump said. But get a chyron ready: I’m calling it, ‘Trump: The Return to Power.’

A half-hour later, Anderson Cooper was indeed at the back of Trump’s 757, awaiting the former President’s exit. The CNN chyron read, Trump: The Return to Power?

Dammit, Trump said inside his plane. Zucker really screwed me with that question mark. Note to self: always demand full creative control of punctuation. It’s all about the punctuation. Anyway, Lou, let’s go.

Sir, um, the airport is not providing a stairway for the exit, Dunkin said. You’ll have to blow the emergency slide.

Not exactly presidential, John, Trump said.

No, sir. But despite 306 electoral votes and a clear win in the popular vote if you take out the illegal ballots, you’re not, technically, President.

A reflex came to life in the form of a vocal thunderclap: “You’re fired.”

Delight surged through Trump as he opened the back door and waved both arms, Nixon-style, to Cooper, waiting below with two camera crews. With the push of a button, the plane’s yellow evacuation slide exploded down to the tarmac.

I begin my return to power right now! Trump hollered before leaping onto the sloping air mattress like a gigantic kid on an amusement park ride — all the while keeping a hand on his head to prevent his hair from lifting like a broken gull’s wing.

Propelled by momentum, Trump zoomed precariously to his feet, walked directly to the camera and pulled a pastel-colored envelope from his pocket. In the top left corner, Trump had crossed out the Mar-a-Lago return address and, instead, had scribbled, President Donald Trump. The letter was addressed to Sen. Hatch and Speaker Ryan.

We all know that Vice President Pence is a man of oversized ambitions and undersized genitals, Trump began. Starting right now, I am taking back our country and restoring to power a man of substantial cojones whose only ambition is to serve the people.

And then he started walking. Certainly, there had been previous footage of President Donald Trump perambulating. He did walk-and-talks with journalists along the West Colonnade, but really only from the Brady Briefing Room to the door of the Oval Office, a distance of roughly 140 feet. In photo ops at factories, Trump would have to walk at least half the assembly line to take credit for a supposedly new investment that was actually announced long before the election. And in a single 18-hole round of golf, Trump could walk a few yards from his golf cart three or four times per hole, plus about 20 feet per green, depending on how bad his putting was that day.

So he certainly could walk a little.

An airport worker had mercifully opened the northernmost gate, allowing Trump to bypass the terminals and save about a half-mile. He, Cooper and the TV crews exited onto Mount Vernon Parkway and started trekking north.
